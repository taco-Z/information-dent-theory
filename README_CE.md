# Information Potential Theory — Core Equations

## 1. Integrated Information Density

[
I(x) = \rho(x) \cdot \mathcal{K}(x)
]

* ( \rho(x) ): information density at position (x)
* ( \mathcal{K}(x) ): integration factor (degree of interdependence)

---

## 2. Integration Factor (Interdependence)

[
\mathcal{K}(x) = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{j=1}^{N}
\left|
\frac{\partial F}{\partial x_j}
\right|
]

* Measures how much local changes affect the whole system
* Represents interdependence strength

---

## 3. Information Potential

[
\Phi(x) \propto I(x)
]

or more explicitly:

[
\Phi(x) = \alpha \cdot I(x)
]

* ( \Phi(x) ): potential (depression in space)
* ( \alpha ): proportional constant

---

## 4. Force (Emergent Gravity)

[
\mathbf{F} = - \nabla \Phi(x)
]

* Force arises from the gradient of the information potential

---

## 5. Extended Radial Model (Galaxy Scale)

[
F(r) \propto \frac{M}{r^2} + \frac{I_{\text{core}} \cdot I_{\text{global}}}{r}
]

* First term: classical gravity
* Second term: information-structure contribution

---

## 6. Complexity Definition

[
C = I \cdot H
]

* ( I ): integrated information (interdependence)
* ( H ): diversity / entropy of states

---

## 7. Node Emergence Condition

[
I(x) > I_{\text{threshold}}
]

* A node forms when integrated information exceeds a critical value

---

## 8. Black Hole Condition

[
I(x) \rightarrow I_{\max}
\quad \Rightarrow \quad
|\nabla \Phi| \rightarrow \infty
]

* Critical saturation of integrated information
* Leads to an effective horizon (information boundary)

---

## 9. Evolution Loop (Discrete Form)

[
C_{t+1} = f(C_t)
]

where:

[
f(C) = C + \beta \cdot C^\gamma
]

* Nonlinear growth of complexity
* Driven by expansion and interdependence

---

## 10. Core Principle

[
\Phi(x) \sim I(x), \quad
\mathbf{F} \sim -\nabla I(x)
]

---
